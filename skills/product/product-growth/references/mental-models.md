## Growth Sequencing & Strategy

| # | Model | Source | Verified Quote | Application |
|---|-------|--------|---------------|-------------|
| 1 | **Retention Before Acquisition** | Elena Verna (`eoy-review` L210) | "Never start with product-led acquisition. You first always have to start with product-led retention, activation." | Always diagnose retention before recommending acquisition |
| 2 | **4 Growth Engines** | Sean Ellis (`sean-ellis` L517) | "There's basically four ways to grow: sales, SEO, virality/word-of-mouth, or paid growth — usually one engine is responsible for almost all of your growth." | Identify dominant engine, don't spread across all 4 |
| 3 | **Growth Team ≠ Growth** | Elena Verna (`elena-verna` L46) | "There is a huge misconception that in order to get growth going, you need a growth team... it's not something you can outsource." | No growth team hire until PMF + data |
| 4 | **S-Curves & 18-Month Cycles** | Elena Verna (`elena-verna` L445) | "Every 18 months you need to introduce something new in order for it to continue evolving." | Audit loop age; flag if >18 months |
| 5 | **95% Innovation for AI** | Elena Verna (`elena-verna-40` L45) | "Only 30-40% of what I've learned transfers here... 95% innovating on growth, 5% optimization." | AI products: innovation-first playbook |
| 21 | **Growth Model = 50% Value in Building** | Dan Hockenmaier (`dan-hockenmaier`) | "50% of the value you get from it is simply building the model. It forces you to understand it." | Build model for understanding, not just forecasting |
| 22 | **Retention Sensitivity > Intuition** | Dan Hockenmaier (`dan-hockenmaier`) | "Your growth is much more sensitive to customer retention than you can ever intuit." | Small retention gains > large acquisition gains |
| 24 | **Kindle → Fire Strategy** | Casey Winters (`casey-winters` L1) | "The goal of your kindle strategies, these non-scalable hacks, they only exist to unlock the fire strategies." | Non-scalable hacks only exist to unlock scalable loops |

## Activation & Onboarding

| # | Model | Source | Verified Quote | Application |
|---|-------|--------|---------------|-------------|
| 6 | **Core Action = Activation = Retention** | Sarah Tavel (`sarah-tavel` L75) | "The core action of that product forms the foundation... if they perform the action, they're very likely to come back." | Define core action first; NUX leads to it |
| 7 | **Speed to Value** | Sean Ellis (Lookout case) | Repositioned on antivirus + streamlined onboarding → 7% to 40% PMF score in 2 weeks | Set right expectations + speed to must-have |
| 8 | **Activation Rate <15% Is Better** | Lauryn Isford (`lauryn-isford` L39) | "An activation rate in a lower percentage range, maybe 5-15%, is better... higher correlation with long-term retention." | Don't chase high activation with weak definition |
| 9 | **Aha → Setup → Habit Moments** | Ben Williams (`ben-williams` L441) | "In the activation process, we have set up moments, aha moments and habit moments." | Design activation as 3-stage pipeline |
| 10 | **Onboarding → Habit → Retention** | Adam Fishman (`adam-fishman` L376) | "Onboarding really drives habit formation with the product, which leads to retention." | Onboarding = building habit, not just "getting started" |
