# Observations

Append-only log of non-trivial interactions, patterns, and feedback.

---

## 2026-03-24 — Phase 9 Eval (CEO x BU Product Workshop)

### Observation 1: Solution-in-Job is the #1 mistake
Teams consistently embed product terms ("danh mục", "widget") in job statements. CEO had to explicitly challenge this. Skill v1.0 had "demand-side first" principle but no concrete detector mechanism.

### Observation 2: AI generates polished but shallow JTBD
80% of teams produced near-identical segmentation when using AI. Polished language masked lack of depth. No irrational elements, no personal stories. Key: AI needs to be challenged with "tao không thỏa mãn" to go deeper.

### Observation 3: Emotional outcome = true Level 1
Product team spent 1 year solving functional jobs ("xem tổng chi") but missed emotional core ("an tâm tài chính"). CEO: "Core job không phải quản lý chi tiêu mà là an tâm tài chính không cần nỗ lực." Huawei health watch analogy sealed this insight.

### Observation 4: Segments must survive product changes
Team 2 discovered: behavior-based segments (Minimalist, Avoider) collapse when features change. Reframed to personality × situation axes. CEO confirmed: personality axis is more stable than financial state axis.

### Observation 5: Widget Paradox in metrics
Good product can REDUCE engagement metrics. Widget đã trả lời câu hỏi → user không cần click thêm. MEU (visits) vô nghĩa. Need metrics that measure "progress achieved" not "features used".

### Observation 6: "Đặt câu hỏi chưa đủ" — humility matters
Product team lead: "Một năm vừa rồi em đặt câu hỏi chưa đủ." CEO highlighted this as the most valuable insight of the session. JTBD starts with asking the right question.

### Observation 7: Mode F (Metric Design) needed
No Mode existed for JTBD → metric translation. Teams struggled most with North Star Metric. Added Mode F in v1.1.
