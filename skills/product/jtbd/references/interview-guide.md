# JTBD Interview Guide — Story Prompts & Techniques

## Before Interview

### Who to Recruit
- People who RECENTLY (last 3 months) bought/switched/churned
- NOT people who "plan to" or "want to" — future intent is unreliable
- For new products: people currently using competitors/adjacent solutions
- Mix: 60% recent buyers, 40% recent churners

### Sample Planning
- **Round 1:** 10 interviews (diverse contexts)
- **Round 2:** 10 interviews (deep-dive into patterns found in Round 1)
- Patterns start repeating at 7-8
- Two rounds of 10 > one round of 20

## During Interview

### Opening (get them talking)
- "Tell me the story of the day you decided to [buy X / leave Y / try Z]..."
- "Walk me through what happened. Start from the very beginning."
- "What was going on in your life/work at that time?"

### Forces Extraction Prompts

**F1 Push (struggling moment):**
- "What wasn't working anymore?"
- "What changed that made you think about this?"
- "Describe the moment you thought 'I need to do something different'"

**F2 Pull (new outcome):**
- "What were you hoping would happen?"
- "What did the ideal look like in your mind?"
- "If this worked perfectly, what would be different?"

**F3 Anxiety (of the new):**
- "What made you hesitate?"
- "What were you worried about?"
- "What almost stopped you from doing it?"

**F4 Habit (of the present):**
- "What were you already using / doing?"
- "What was hard about leaving that behind?"
- "What did you have to give up?"

### Deepening Techniques

**3 Layers of Language:**
1. **Pablum** ("It was fine") → Push: "What specifically was fine about it?"
2. **Fantasy/Nightmare** ("It was TERRIBLE!") → Ground: "Can you give me the specific moment?"
3. **Actual Story** ("Here's what happened...") → This is gold. Stay here.

**Edge of Language:**
When they run out of words → bracket:
"Was it more about [X] or [Y]?" (intentionally neither — forces new vocabulary)

**Play Back Wrong:**
"So you bought it because of the discount?" (when you know it wasn't)
→ They say "No!" → elaborate on the REAL reason

**Timeline Reconstruction:**
- "When was the FIRST time you thought about this?"
- "How long between thinking about it and actually doing it?"
- People say "a week" — usually 6-18 months

### What to Listen For
- Trade-offs they made (what they gave up)
- Who else was involved in the decision
- What they tried before (the fired solutions)
- The irrational part (seems crazy but context explains it)
- Emotional/social dimensions (not just functional)

## After Interview

### Extracting Artifacts
For each interview, document:
1. **Struggling moment** (F1 trigger)
2. **Desired outcome** (F2 vision)
3. **Anxieties** (F3 hesitations)
4. **Habits** (F4 what they left behind)
5. **Hire criteria** (why THIS solution)
6. **Fire criteria** (what would make them leave)
7. **Timeline** (first thought → purchase → use)
8. **Trade-offs** (what they gave up)
9. **Energy dimensions** (functional/emotional/social signals)

### Clustering (not Segmenting)
- Group interviews by PATHWAY (similar push+pull combos), not demographics
- Usually find 3-5 distinct pathways
- Look for conflicts between pathways
- Name each pathway by the progress being made

## Red Flags in Interviews
- Interviewee only talks about features → redirect to struggling moment
- "I always wanted X" → probe: "What happened that made you ACTUALLY do it?"
- No trade-offs mentioned → too smooth, missing data
- All answers functional → push for emotional/social dimensions
