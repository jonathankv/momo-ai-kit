# When JTBD Doesn't Work — Limitations & Counter-Arguments

## Situations Where JTBD Is Not The Right Tool

| Situation | Why JTBD fails | What to do instead | Source |
|-----------|----------------|-------------------|--------|
| **No real choice** | Health insurance from employer — no trade-off decision → no visible job | Ethnography, friction analysis, usage study | Bob Moesta ep.1 (line 439) |
| **Deep habit** | Chewing gum — can't remember buying, only chewing | Study usage moments, not purchase decisions | Bob Moesta ep.1 (line 451) |
| **Network effects** | Facebook PYMK: "performing job for THEM, not YOU" | Consider system-level jobs, not just individual | Sriram Krishnan (line 39) |
| **Framework-as-religion** | Twitter: "followed to such a religious extent it's just unhelpful" | Balance with judgment + product taste | Kayvon Beykpour (line 325) |
| **Metrics vs customer** | Amazon order confirmation: great for metrics, terrible for customers | Don't let JTBD framework override product taste | Kayvon Beykpour (line 334) |
| **Zero-to-one (no behavior)** | Nobody uses anything similar yet | Interview adjacent solution users: "What will people STOP using?" | Bob Moesta ep.1 (line 253) |

## Counter-Arguments (documented, not dismissed)

### Sriram Krishnan: "I hate JTBD"
**Argument:** "No successful company has ever been built on top of JTBD." Facebook PYMK example — makes YOUR experience worse to make THEIR experience better. No single-user JTBD explains this.

**Bob Moesta rebuttal:** Autobooks, Southern NH University (200K students), Facebook Marketplace, Intercom, Basecamp all used JTBD. For zero-to-one: study what people would STOP using.

**Synthesis:** Sriram's critique is about network-effect products where system-level optimization ≠ individual JTBD. Valid edge case, not a total invalidation.

### Kayvon Beykpour: "Every framework at its limit is unhelpful"
**Argument:** At Twitter, OKRs + JTBD led to optimizing DAU at expense of user experience (reverse chronological timeline toggle). "The answer isn't any one framework. Sometimes it's just good old-fashioned judgment and product taste."

**Synthesis:** JTBD is a lens, not a religion. It's most powerful when combined with product judgment. It should inform decisions, not replace them.

### Paul Adams (Intercom): "Use what helps, don't overthink"
**Argument:** "Unless you're an academic... just use the thing that helps you improve lives in some small meaningful way. That's the goal."

**Synthesis:** The Four Forces is the most portable element of JTBD. Can be used standalone without the full framework. Intercom founders literally used it to recruit Paul from Facebook.

## 3 Schools of JTBD — Know The Difference

| School | Champion | Approach | Best for |
|--------|----------|----------|----------|
| **Moesta/Christensen** | Bob Moesta | Demand-side, qualitative, story-based, context+outcome | Startups, discovery, zero-to-one |
| **Ulwick (ODI)** | Tony Ulwick | Supply-side, quantitative, importance×satisfaction | Large orgs, regulated, systematic |
| **Conference-room** | Nobody (anti-pattern) | Hypothesize without customer interviews | Never |

Bob Moesta: "Only people have jobs, products don't." Ulwick: "What functions can our product perform?" Different starting points, both have value in different contexts.

## Key Rule
When unsure if JTBD applies → ask: "Can the customer make a CHOICE?" If yes → JTBD works. If no → use other methods.
