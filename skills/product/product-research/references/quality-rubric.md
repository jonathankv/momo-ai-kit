# Quality Rubric — Product Research Output Evaluation

Use this rubric for Mode F (Research Synthesis) and self-evaluation of research quality.

---

## 8-Dimension Scoring (1-5 scale)

| Dimension | 1 (Poor) | 3 (Adequate) | 5 (Excellent) |
|-----------|----------|---------------|----------------|
| **Evidence Quality** | Claims without sources | Most claims sourced | Every claim traced to specific evidence |
| **Falsifiability** | Designed to confirm | Some disconfirming evidence sought | Explicitly tested against alternatives |
| **Source Diversity** | Single source/method | 2-3 sources, 1 method | Multiple sources, mixed methods, triangulated |
| **Actionability** | "Interesting findings" | Recommendations present | Specific actions tied to business decisions |
| **Behavioral Grounding** | Based on stated preferences | Mix of stated + observed | Grounded in actual behavior + context |
| **Altitude Appropriateness** | Wrong altitude for question | Correct altitude, partial execution | Right altitude, complete methodology |
| **Bias Awareness** | No bias acknowledged | Common biases noted | Specific biases identified + mitigated |
| **Stakeholder Clarity** | Unclear who acts on this | Decision-maker identified | Clear owner + decision + timeline |

---

## Scoring Guide

- **≥4.0 avg:** High-quality research. Ready to inform decisions.
- **3.0-3.9 avg:** Adequate. Identify specific gaps and address.
- **<3.0 avg:** Significant gaps. Do not use for high-stakes decisions without improvement.
- **Any dimension <2.0:** Critical gap. Must address before proceeding.

---

## Common Quality Issues

| Issue | Detection | Fix |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| Confirmation bias | All evidence supports hypothesis | Actively seek disconfirming evidence |
| Sample bias | Only talked to power users | Include churned users, non-users, edge cases |
| Recency bias | Only recent data considered | Check historical trends, seasonal patterns |
| Survivorship bias | Only studying successes | Include failed attempts, abandoned workflows |
| Leading questions | Responses match expectations too well | Review interview recordings, look for leading |
| Small N fallacy | Strong conclusions from 2-3 interviews | Note sample size, frame as hypothesis, not conclusion |

---

## Research Quality Checklist

Before presenting research findings:

- [ ] Can every claim be traced to a specific data point?
- [ ] Did we look for evidence AGAINST our hypothesis?
- [ ] Are sources diverse (≥3 distinct sources/methods)?
- [ ] Would a skeptical stakeholder find this convincing?
- [ ] Is the sample representative of our target users?
- [ ] Are we acknowledging what we DON'T know?
- [ ] Are recommendations specific enough to act on?
- [ ] Is there a clear "what happens next"?
