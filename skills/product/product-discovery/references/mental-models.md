## Discovery Process & Framing

| # | Model | Source | Verified Quote | Application |
|---|-------|--------|---------------|-------------|
| 1 | **Opportunity Solution Tree** | Teresa Torres (`teresa-torres` L108) | ✅ "It starts with an outcome at the root of the tree, and then it branches into the opportunity space and then it branches into solutions, and maybe even assumption tests from there." | Structure discovery: Outcome → Opportunities (needs/pains) → Solutions → Assumption Tests. Don't skip layers. |
| 2 | **Opportunities ≠ Solutions** | Teresa Torres (`teresa-torres` L117) | ✅ "98% of people that write opportunities write them as solutions." | When framing opportunities, test: is this describing a user need/pain, or a feature? If it could be built, it's a solution, not an opportunity. |
| 3 | **Structure via Experience Map** | Teresa Torres (`teresa-torres` L129) | ✅ "I recommend teams structure the opportunity space using an experience map... what's the overall experience of trying to [goal]?" | Map the user journey first, then capture needs/pains at each stage. Competitors' opportunity spaces look similar — differentiation is in which you pursue and how you solve. |
| 4 | **Smaller Opportunities = Solvable** | Teresa Torres (`teresa-torres` L156-162) | ✅ "As we work our way down the tree, we get to smaller and smaller opportunities... things that we can actually address." | Decompose big evergreen problems into specific solvable sub-problems. "I can't find something to watch" → "I can't tell if this show is any good" → "Who's the cast?" |
| 5 | **Fall in Love with the Problem** | Uri Levine (`uri-levine` L43) | ✅ "Fall in love, fall in love, fall in love, fall in love with the problem" | When you or user starts with solution, redirect: "What problem? Who suffers? How many?" |
| 6 | **Clarity Over Correctness** | Tomer Cohen (`tomer-cohen`) | ✅ "We might be wrong, but we are not confused." | Prioritize clear problem definition over having the right answer. Disagree openly. |
| 7 | **Understand → Identify → Execute** | Bangaly Kaba (`bangaly-kaba` L49) | ✅ "understand, identify, execute" | Anti-pattern: Identify → Justify → Execute. Always invest in understanding before identifying opportunities. |
| 8 | **Eigenquestions** | Shishir Mehrotra (`shishir-mehrotra` L330) | ✅ Extensive discussion with YouTube/Apple examples | When many questions compete, find the one that eliminates most others when answered. |

## Interviewing & User Research

| # | Model | Source | Verified Quote | Application |
|---|-------|--------|---------------|-------------|
| 9 | **Interview by Story, Not Questions** | Teresa Torres (`teresa-torres` L174-180) | ✅ "If I ask you, like, 'Tell me about the last time you watched a movie,' now I can collect things like where were you and who were you with... I'm going to get answers to all those direct questions, but it's grounded in this specific instance." | Don't ask "What do you like?" Ask "Tell me about the last time you [did X]." Collect stories, not opinions. |
| 10 | **Hear Needs Users Don't Know** | Teresa Torres (`teresa-torres` L183) | ✅ "I might hear needs that you're not even aware of. We're so used to everything being mediocre, we're not even aware of a lot of these needs." | Stories reveal unconscious needs. Direct questions only surface conscious ones. |
| 11 | **Be Curious, Not Prepared** | Teresa Torres (`teresa-torres` L420-426) | ✅ "We're so worried about asking the next question, we stop listening to the interviewee. We just missed everything we were told." | Don't prepare 50 questions. Ask one opening question, then follow the story with "What happened next?" Stay shallow = lose value. |
| 12 | **Behavior Over Intent** | Teresa Torres (`teresa-torres` L449-450) | ✅ "Product people are in the business of changing behavior... they focus on what people would do, on what people think... It's a garbage in, garbage out situation." | Never ask "Would you use X?" Ask "Tell me what you actually did." Past behavior > hypothetical intent. |
| 13 | **One Interview > Zero** | Teresa Torres (`teresa-torres` L471-480) | ✅ "Every human in business is making decisions with zero data. So I'm going to go with one is better than zero." | When stakeholders question small sample sizes: you're held to a standard nobody else meets. One is better than zero, and continuous feedback loops compound. |

## Validation & Testing

| # | Model | Source | Verified Quote | Application |
|---|-------|--------|---------------|-------------|
| 14 | **MVP = Test Hypothesis, Not Ship Crappy** | Eric Ries (`eric-ries` L186) | ✅ "MVP is simply for whatever the hypothesis is that we're trying to test, what is the most efficient way to get the validation we need?" | MVP quality depends on context. Jet engine MVP took 10 years. iOS app MVP might take 2 weeks. Minimum = minimum to test THIS hypothesis. |
| 15 | **Cut Features, Not Quality** | Eric Ries (`eric-ries` L270) | ✅ "Write out the list of features that are necessary in your MVP. Cut it in half and cut it in half again and build that." | When bar is high, do fewer things at high quality. Don't do many things at low quality. |
| 16 | **Assumption Tests Beat Whole-Idea Tests** | Teresa Torres (`teresa-torres` L495-498) | ✅ "We have to learn how to take an idea and break it into its underlying assumptions... run tests that are small enough that they're just testing that assumption." | Don't test the whole product. Break into assumptions, prioritize, test individually. Faster, cheaper, more learning. |
| 17 | **Discovery in Parallel, Not Before Delivery** | Teresa Torres (`teresa-torres` L279-288) | ✅ "Everything in our backlog is a bet, everything. Whether we do discovery or not... Keep making bets. In parallel, start doing some discovery." | Don't stop delivery to do discovery. Run both continuously. Discovery bets get better over time. |
| 18 | **Carve Out to Prove** | Tomer Cohen (`tomer-cohen`) | ✅ LinkedIn carved out 2M users to prove strategy | Don't try to change everything. Carve out small cohort, prove dramatic results, then expand. |

## Decision Making & Evidence

| # | Model | Source | Verified Quote | Application |
|---|-------|--------|---------------|-------------|
| 19 | **Compare and Contrast Options** | Teresa Torres (`teresa-torres` L337-342) | ✅ "When you're looking for a place to live, you don't look at one apartment... If you're feeling like this needs to be a really good solution... you need to increase your options." | Work with 3+ solution options for important problems. Single-option discovery is just validation theater. |
| 20 | **Diagnose with Data, Treat with Design** | Julie Zhuo (`julie-zhuo` L61) | ✅ "Diagnose with data and treat with design. Data is not a tool that's going to tell you what you should build." | Data shows WHERE the problem is. Creative process shows HOW to solve it. |
| 21 | **Believe 100% Until Data Says No** | Gustav Söderström (`gustav-söderström` L340) | ✅ "You have to believe in things 100% until the data says no and then you believe in something else 100%." | Commit fully to hypothesis. But when evidence contradicts, update immediately. Don't defend sunk investment. |
| 22 | **Empowered Team = Outcome, Not Output** | Marty Cagan (`marty-cagan-20` L151-154) | ✅ "Instead of being given that roadmap of features, they're given problems to solve... the measure is not ship the thing. The measure is it solves the problem." | Discovery only matters in outcome-oriented teams. Feature teams don't need discovery — they need better problems to solve. |

## Evidence Hierarchy

From corpus synthesis across multiple sources:

```
Behavioral data (what users actually do)
  > Interview stories (what they actually did, in context)
    > Survey data (self-reported, out of context)
      > Stakeholder intuition (opinion without evidence)
        > "Users want X" (hearsay, no attribution)
```

Always tag evidence by source type when synthesizing research.

## Discovery at Scale

| # | Model | Source | Verified Quote | Application |
|---|-------|--------|---------------|-------------|
| 23 | **Four Criteria for Opportunity Ranking** | Chandra Janakiraman (`chandra-janakiraman` L268-280) | ✅ Expected impact, certainty of impact, clarity of levers, differentiation | Rank opportunities on 4 axes. "Certainty of impact is how concrete the evidence is." Qualitative scoring (H/M/L) is valid when data is lacking. |
| 24 | **Down-Select to Exactly Three** | Chandra Janakiraman (`chandra-janakiraman` L268) | ✅ "Down select from those 10 to 15 opportunity areas into ideally three" | From 10-15 clusters, force 3 pillars. Creates clarity and focus. |
| 25 | **Lighthouse Users (10→100→1000)** | Tanguy Crusson (`tanguy-crusson` L625-640) | ✅ "Everything that explains why we believe they are a proxy for every customer" | Staged validation: 10 users = prove problems. 100 = test variations. 1000 = solve self-service gaps. Each stage has distinct goals. |
| 26 | **Pre-Code Demand Validation** | Tanguy Crusson (`tanguy-crusson` L247-250) | ✅ "Before we brought a single line of code... in two weeks, we got more than 3000 signups" | Before building: ad in newsletter + landing page + waitlist. Validate demand before writing code. |
| 27 | **Strategy Has No Intrinsic Value** | Chandra Janakiraman (`chandra-janakiraman` L697) | ✅ "Intrinsically strategy has no business value. It's basically a document with a few words." | Strategy only accumulates value through execution results. Same pillars led to opposite outcomes at Meta (Oculus vs Portal). |
| 28 | **Wonder → Explore → Make → Impact** | Tanguy Crusson (`tanguy-crusson` L460-476) | ✅ Atlassian Point A incubation framework | Stage gates: Wonder (prove problem + why now), Explore (validate solutions via Figma), Make (alpha/beta/GA), Impact (measure results). |

## Validation & Pivots

| # | Model | Source | Verified Quote | Application |
|---|-------|--------|---------------|-------------|
| 29 | **PMF Litmus Test** | Eric Ries (`eric-ries` L438) | ✅ "If you're asking whether you should pivot or not you probably know the answer already." | If you have time to ask about PMF, you probably don't have it. When it's working, there's no time for navel-gazing. |
| 30 | **Fixed Time-Box for Decisive Action** | Eric Ries (`eric-ries` L441-447) | ✅ "Give yourself a fixed period of time to take some decisive action and see if it feels better." | 6 weeks, 100% focus on the one thing that matters. If diminishing returns persist (10% → 10.5% → 10.6%), it's time to pivot. |
| 31 | **Don't Eat Your Own Bullshit** | Tanguy Crusson (`tanguy-crusson` L220) | ✅ "Don't eat your own bullshit... we don't try to make things sound smarter than they are." | Validate WHY past success happened before assuming it transfers. HipChat assumed Atlassian's bottom-up playbook would work for comms. It didn't. |
| 32 | **Hiding Behind Research** | Tanguy Crusson (`tanguy-crusson` L646) | ✅ "Hiding behind research and not being close enough to the ground with customers." | Formal UXR/CSAT is not a substitute for knowing 10 customers by name. Engineers who meet users become product engineers. |
